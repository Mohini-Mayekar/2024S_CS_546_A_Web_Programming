My Travel Destinations

Delhi, India

Delhi, the capital of India, is a fascinating fusion of many cultures and offers amazing cuisine in addition to a lengthy history. Since Delhi is a city built upon cities, it is home to numerous historical sites and monuments from various ages. This wealth of old history and culture, includes one of the biggest mosques in India, Jama Masjid, the Red Fort from the 17th century, which had an influence on many later structures in north India, Humayun's Tomb, Laxmi Narayan Temple, and several more mosques, historical figure tombs, and temples.
Delhi is referred to as the culinary capital of India since it has one of the liveliest food scenes in the nation. When visiting the city, you should make sure to sample some of the well-known Delhi cuisine, whether it is at a neighborhood eatery or a street vendor. Must-have foods include kulfi, samosas, dal makhani, kebabs, and butter chicken. For the most enriching experience, it is recommended to do a food tour of Old Delhi, which combines history, culture, and cuisine.

Kerala, India

Kerala is the most gorgeous state in the eyes of many tourists, with breathtaking scenery that includes sunny beaches, lush hills, and shimmering backwaters. The state's most popular attraction is the well-known Kerala backwaters, with Alleppey serving as their center. Photographers dream of capturing this serene, enchanting network of rivers, lagoons, canals, and waterways lined by palm trees.
A traditional houseboat, formerly used by the people to transport fish, rice, and spices, is the ideal way to explore the backwaters; it's now been converted into a "floating hotel." You can eat freshly prepared Indian cuisine while observing the passing scenery while spending one or two nights at sea.

  2. Munnar
  3. Kovalam

Amritsar, India

Amritsar, which is located on India's northwest border with Pakistan, provides a unique tourist experience. It is home to the magnificent Golden Temple (Sri Harmandir Sahib, "The Abode of God"), which serves as the main Sikh place of worship. The temple draws pilgrims from all around the world and welcomes guests of all faiths.
The temple features entrances on all four sides, signifying openness, and the dome is coated in gold. The temple appears magnificent at night, especially with its golden dome lit up. Thus, the evening is a perfect time to take in all of its charm and beauty. In addition to its spectacular exterior, the Golden Temple is renowned for housing the world's largest free communal kitchen, which provides up to 100,000 guests with free vegetarian meals every day. You can take a look around the kitchen and even offer to help make and serve food.
